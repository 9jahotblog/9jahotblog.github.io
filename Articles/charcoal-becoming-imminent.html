Use of Charcoal becoming imminent due to the increase in gas prices!

Peter Sonibare

Everything that is wrong with Nigeria's economy is explained by the ongoing increase in Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG/cooking gas) prices, which is why our economic managers should make a conscious effort to foster an environment of optimism. For many Nigerians, both those who live in urban areas and those in rural regions, cooking gas is beneficial and helpful for their daily needs. Nigeria is fortunate to have access to LPG, with the ninth-largest resource in the world as of 2019 (about 207 trillion standard cubic feet).

Nigerians have recently,

switched from traditional cooking techniques like using firewood or charcoal to more contemporary ones like using cooking gas. It is clear that Nigerians are reverting to the traditional manner of cooking as a result of the steady increase in cooking gas prices. We all know that people are turning back to the traditional ways of cooking because of the rising cost of cooking gas, despite claims to the contrary that it is cheaper or faster. Charcoal's low cost is still a key consideration for households when choosing their fuel source. Given that kerosene, Liquid Petroleum Gas (LPG), and electricity are more expensive than charcoal Many African countries have reported that the production of charcoal is one of the main factors contributing to deforestation and forest degradation. This is because woods and forest areas are necessary for the production of charcoal in order to satisfy the same energy requirements as firewood.

However,

due to the sustained production potential of trees, proper regulation of the production of charcoal can help the local population financially. Gases and particulate matter (PM) are frequently released into working and residential environments when charcoal is burned in homes. The volatile compounds released during the processing and burning of charcoal, such as particulate matter (PM), carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, sulfate oxides, and others, may cause respiratory issues and eventually diseases like acute respiratory infection (ARI), chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, and others.

A caterer,

Mrs Kehinde Adeola, known as Iya Ibeji, who lives at Ayobo, when asked about the use of charcoal prior to the increase in cooking gas, said "Normally, i use gas sometimes, I use charcoal to cook for my customers, but now due to the increase in cooking gas, I result to using charcoal because it is cheaper although, it is not that convenient for me but I just have to use it because of the economic situation". Mrs Adenike Damilola who sells charcoal at Ayobo market, said "Usually, a bag of charcoal goes for N3,000 depending on the season and quality of the wood. Now, a bag of charcoal goes for at least N5,000 some say it is because of the increase in the price of gas, some say it is because of deforestation, nowadays, I've been making sales than I used to as, people are now using charcoal to cook cause it is cheaper although, the price might skyrocket soon".
