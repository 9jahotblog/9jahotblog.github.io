TOP 7 DEADLIEST DISEASES...Jan 22, 2023

Diseases are illnesses affecting humans, animals, or plants, often caused by infection. One must have been affected by any disease and still survives as some diseases are curable while some are not, therefore, some diseases are more deadly than others.

Aside from the epidemic plague, which severe impact is short-termed, other lethal diseases are reducing the worldâ€™s population on an annual basis. Here is the list of the top seven (7) deadliest. Keep reading through to know them and how to treat the diseases. Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): is sometimes called coronary heart or ischemic heart disease is the blockage of the coronary arteries caused by plaque accumulation. This condition limits the flow of oxygen to the heart. CAD has caused a million death globally in an annual estimation.

Symptoms: stable angina, shortness of breath, chest pain , heart racing, hot temperature etc. Remedy: Exercise, limit alcohol, eat healthy , avoid smoking or intake of any other tobacco products, rather take medication to lower your blood pressure , cholesterol to reduce the risk of blood clot and to manage stable angina. Surgery is also recommended. Lower Respiratory Tract Infection.

These are diseases that affects the lungs or vocal box. They includes Pneumonia, Bronchitis, and Tuberculosis Symptoms :Runny nose, dry cough, low fever, sore throat, dull headache, chest pain, difficulty in inhaling and exhaling, fever, etc. Remedy: medications for a cough or fever, rest and drinking plenty of fluids. Malignant Tumors: These are cancerous cell growth and spreading abnormally causing harm to nearby tissues. Symptoms: Lump under the skin, weight changes, yellowish, darkening or redness of the skin and sores. Remedy: Take medication that destroys cancer cells, exposure to high energy rays kills cancer cells and destroys cellsâ€™ growth hormones.

Diabetes: World Health Organization, WHO defines diabetes as a chronic, metabolic disease characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (or blood sugar), which leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys, and nerves. Symptoms: Frequent urinating, thirst, loss of weight, dehydration, blurry vision, numb or tingling hands or feet and fatigue. Remedy: Insulin, exercise, diet. Alzheimer's disease: This is a disease that causes a malfunction in the brain leading to other dementias. Symptoms: forgetfulness, inability to think, judge, and make decisions, memory loss. Remedy: regular exercises, quit alcohol and smoking, follow treatment to manage diabetes and High Blood Pressure.

Diarrheal Disease: Caused by viruses, bacteria, and parasites, this disease is characterized by the frequent release of watery stool culminating in the loss of weight, nausea, dehydration, etc Symptoms: stomach pain, abdominal cramps, weight loss, fever, blood or pus in stool, frequent vomiting. Remedy: wash your hands frequently, watch what you eat, medication, and drink safe and clean water. Stroke: This is the total numbness of one side of the body usually caused by an interruption of blood supply to part of the brain, obstructing the brain from getting enough oxygen and nutrients. Symptoms: trouble walking, speaking, and understanding what other people say, paralysis of some parts of the body, inability to see clearly. Remedy: medication, ask the person to imitate your speech, walking steps, and smile, massage, eat healthy, exercise regularly.
