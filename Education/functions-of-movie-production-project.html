FUNCTIONS/ROLE OF MOVIE PRODUCTION PROJECT

LET'S KICKSTART WITH THE PRODUCER

-He Finances the movie.

-The producer gathers investments.

-He reaches out to actors for leading roles.

-He Arranges a team to manage business operations.

-He Supervises the production.

-Works with the director.

-Assigns a cinematographer, a production designer, & casting agents.

-Determines how much the production will cost.

-Setting up the rehearsals & schedules.

-Selects cast members.

-Secures funding for the production.

DIRECTOR

-He reviews and interprets the script.

-Works with other departments on set.

-Works with casting, camera & design.

-Selects assistant directors.

-Creates the film's tone.

-Approves the film's production budgets.

-Works with editors.

-Works with other departments to produce the final version of the film.

-Writes the script, makes comments, edits, or changes the script.

-Works with the actors during rehearsals.

-Happy with takes before moving to the next scene.

ASSOCIATE DIRECTOR

-Ensures daily progress in the film production.

-Arranges the health care of the crew.

-Maintaining wellness & order among the crew.

-Prepares daily to-do or call sheets.

-Checks the cast & crew.

-Estimates how long a scene would take.

-Shortens a scene running a few pages long.

-Serves as a backstage manager.

-Supervises the second, third, & assistant director trainees.

-Basic knowledge and understanding of all the various departments found on a film set.

-Must have smooth communication skills.

-Plans the filming schedule.

-Works with the director, & other heads of the department to ensure a perfect shoot.

CAMERA OPERATOR

-Controls camera equipments.

-Sets up equipments.

-Works accordingly to the camera scripts.

-Creates actions (framing & capturing).

-Responding instantaneously to directions.

-Capture & records all aspects & scenes of the film production.

-Follows the principles of staging & lightning.

-Adjust camera controls & printers.

-Works on camera movements, lightning requirements, and filming.

-Arranges the Studio to the state that best suites the setting of the camera.

-Test, maintain, & clean all broadcasting equipments.

-Works with other lighting directors.

LIGHTNING DIRECTOR

-They "paint the scene" with placements of lights.

-Determines how much electricity needed to light the scene.

-Paints with light or absence of light.

-Creates the colour or mood of filming.

-Uses variety of lighting effects.

-Reduces form & colour.

-Ensures integral lightning for set design.

-Installation & operation of lightning.

-Colour vision.

-Planning the stage before the scene.

AUDIO ENGINEER

-Maintains technical equipment for sound.

-Records the sounds in set or location.

-Mixes & balances the sounds of the pre-recorded audio.

-Get directions from the sound supervisor & abide to it.

-Adjust & position the sound equipment.

-Communicate with other production staff to ensure sound requirements.

-Apply technical knowledge for determination of artistic objectives.

-Record sounds into hard disk or audio tape.

-Abide to all relevant safety precautions.

-Repair sound equipment.

-Monitor & detect sounds.

SOUND RECORDISTS

-Records live sound..

-Capture according to the director's vision.

-They must read the scripts of the film so as to determine the equipment to utilize & it's technical requirements.

-Plan in detail before the shoot..

-Responsible for all sound operations.

-Manage equipments & budgets.

-Interact with other departments.

-Set & placement of mics.

-Works on issues of background noise.

-Works with a sound supervisor.

GRAPHIC DESIGNER

-Works with other members to produce effective content.

-Designs visual concept.

-Creates illustration & logos.

-Create labels to portray they are from a certain time. (90's film. e t.c)

-Creates guidelines on how the logo's & illustration will be used.

-Helps make design choices.

-plays an Integral part of story telling.

VIDEO EDITOR

-Responsible for turning footages from a film into a finished project.

-Studying film scripts.

-Guides scene development.

-Coordinating sound and visual effects in post-production.

-Put together the visions of the director's script. Experience the story as it is recorded on film from the Director‚Äôs point of view.

-Edit frame by frame as needed to complete the film.

-Interact with appropriate people for the vision, goal & editing.

-Manages camera footage.

-Manages Sound effects.

-Manages Graphics to produce a final film or movie.

ART DIRECTOR

-Reports to the production designer.

-Designs the set.

-Coordinate the artists & construction workers..

-Helps determine the overall look of a film or movie production.

-Set props & graphics to create visual identity of the film.

-Supervises the models. Characters, creatures & sets.

-Works with the teams who builds & design sets.

SET DESIGNER

-Creating & designing sets in Programmes & In theatre appearance.

-Working with costume designers, directors & producers.

-Preparing the cost of set.

-Planning.

-Managing budgets.

-Discussing production requirements with the producer & director.

-Monitors design budget.

-Laying out ideas involving the production.

-Researching effects.

-Interacts with costume designer, lights & sound directors.

SOUND PRODUCER

-Obtains all sound effects.

-Sets up sound playbacks.

-Discuss with the director sound Design for the show.

-Selects musical effects.

-Sets up sound reinforcement.

-Attends all rehearsals.

-Ensure the board operator is trained.

-Familiar with computer editing.

- Must attend all production meetings and specific meetings with the Director.

COSTUME DESIGNER

-Designs the costume for production.

-Being practical for the performer.

-Reads the full script & notes the place needing costume.

-Research the setting & time period of the play.

-Researches the fashion for the time period, and places.

-Designs the costume for each character.

-Communicate & present ideas.

-Competent in sewing.

-Ability to work alone as well as with other departments relating to the play.

MAKEUP ARTIST

-Designs the characters look.

-Apply makeup products on actor's body & faces.

-Creates special make up effects.

-Coordinates with lightning & film directors.

-Store make up tools.

-Interact with the production team for tools needed.

-Responsible for make up style consistency throughout the shooting.

-Apply long lasting cosmetics.

-Apply make up according to each character's description.

-Calculate the time required for make up & inform the production team.

EXECUTIVE PRODUCER

-Supervision of the performance. Web series, television show or film.

-Writes the script.

-Thinks of ideas & concepts.

-Assuring production quality.

-Meets the project competitive goals.

-Supervises other producers.

-Maintaining the approval & budget schedules.

-Secures the financing for the production.

-Lead all departments from top.

-Acts as the figure head.

-Takes ownership of decision & outcomes.

CASTING DIRECTOR

-Find the stars to bring to the characters to life.

-Responsible for matching actors to roles.

-Reads the script & meet with producers to know the type of person they're looking for.

-Negotiate actors deal, points & conditions which they put into casting advice note or deal memo.

-Attend theatre productions and graduation shows to spot potential actors.

-Considers actors availability & fees.

-Study the script to understand speaking roles.

-Prepare casting budgets.

CONTENT MARKETER

-Creating & planning valuable content to attract customers into repeat buyers.

-Knowing the audience & what they can do for them...

-Creates campaign to bring a TV drama to the audience attention.

-Mediator between the business they represent and the interests of a specific audience.

-Responsible for sharing content to grow the readership and relationship for the company they represent.

SCRIPT WRITER

-Creates details of the script based on his concepts.

-Create stories for production with understanding of the storyline & target audience.

-Works with the production team revising the script to suit audiences.

-Ensures regulatory requirements are followed.

-Knowledge to translate scripts.

-Ensure there's no copyright issues with the script.

-Identify research sources to assist scriptwriting process.

-Writes the narrative script.

-Revise the written materials.

-Make researches to understand the characters.

PRODUCTION MANAGER

-Responsible for shooting schedules.

-Responsible for managing the day-to-day business side of a production.

-Makes budgetary decisions.

-Organize all the necessary needs of a production staff.

-Prepares documents & permits.

-Assists in daily production tasks.

-Ensures the schedules for production are met.

-Hires catering services.

-Schedules meetings.

-Hires workers & assign people to daily task.

PRODUCTION ASSISTANT.

-Prints & distributes script.

-Run errands for producers & directors.

-Ensures production runs smoothly to finish.

-Helps with set lights equipments & props.

-Assists directors & costume designers.

-Distributes & prints daily paperwork.

DIRECTOR OF PHOTOGRAPHY.

-Controls everything the camera captures.

-Controls lightning, filters & camera movements.

-He is the head of the camera and lighting crews on set.

-Selects the lenses & filters to be used on shoot.

-Plays an Integral part in the story telling process.

-Creates the look of the film.

-Introduces ideas and concepts the director may not have considered.

-Reads the screenplay & discusses with the director the look of the film.

STUNT COORDINATOR

-Plans dangerous scenes & skilled movements on movies..

-Ensures everyone on set remains safe.

-Responsible for making action scenes look realistic.

-Direct complex feats, so actors can perform the stunts in realistic & appealing way.

-Choreographs the stunt to suit the script & director's vision.

-Ensures adequate rehearsals.

VISUAL EFFECTS PRODUCER

-Puts together the team of VFX artists and other technical staff.

-Set the schedules for the work.

-Works with the live action production team.

-Works with the editor in post-production.

-They set the schedules for the work and they manage the budget.

-They communicate between the editor and crew.

SFX ARTIST

-Creates effects that aren't possible by mere filming.

-(Most of marvel movies e.g Advengers were work of talented special effects artists).

-Creating a different world in which amazing things can happen. Things like beasts..

-SFX is applied either on set or before shooting a scene.

-VFX are added in post-production.

-VFX is via graphic design programs, and computer tools.

-SFX is trickery, convincing the audience of the authencity of the story's world.
