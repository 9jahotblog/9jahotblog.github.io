Parents, Children Share their ordeal & experiences with each other...Jan 8, 2023

"You know children, these days they can be very funny and you know how adamant they can be, but most times you don't need to be harsh on them". Mrs. Aina said..

Mrs. Aina who is not just a parent but also a counselor stated that the method used in training her while growing up as a child has not been the method used in training her child(Ren).

She said that children need to be treated with love and care and with the same hands they are to be scolded for their wrong doing. Statistics have shown that children brought up with love and affection and not fear tends to have a better way of modesty than those brought up with fear, to back this up, Sandra Afolayan explains how easy it is to tell her father every details and how she finds it easy to correct herself when her father talks to her, but would always be scared to talk to her mum for fear of been beaten.

Mr. Sunday explained that it depends on the way the family wants to train a child(Ren), whether with the rod or with the words of the mouth. Many parents have come up with several ways in which children should be treated but a lot of children stick with the idea that if treated without fear, they could be better children in the world at large. Gbemileke, A student of the University of Lagos, said she doesn't like the way she's being treated by her dad, she said that her father doesn't care about her own ambition, and that everything evolves around him.

She further explained her pain as she can't express her feelings to him, making it worse, she said, "mum also supports him", she doesn't also like the fact that her father took the step of her grandfather in training her. "It's important to note that these children are the future and whatever we do now will determine how they would be when they grow and even when they give birth", Miss. Seun stated.
