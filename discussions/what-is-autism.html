WHAT IS AUTISM??

Autism or autism spectrum disorder is a broad term used to describe a group of neurodevelopmental conditions. These conditions are characterized by challenges with social skills, repetitive behaviors, speech and non-verbal communication. ASD is found in people around the world regardless of race, ethicality, culture or economic background.

Because autism is a spectrum disorder,

each person with autism has a distinct set of strengths and challenges. The ways in which people with autism learn, think ad problem-solve can vary from highly skilled to severely challenged some people with ASD may require significant support in their daily lives while other may need less support and in some case live entirely independently {just like Shaun from the ‚ÄúGOOD DOCTOR‚Äù}.

Several factors may influence the development of autism

and it is often accompanied by sensory sensitivities and medical issues such as gastrointestinal disorders, seizures, sleep disorders as well as mental health challenges such as anxiety, depression and attention issues,

The DMS-5 divides symptoms of ASD into the following categories:

Problems with communication and social interactions.

To be diagnosed with autism a person must experience symptoms in both of these categories.

Restricted or repetitive patterns of behavior or activities.

Autism also includes symptoms related to body movements and behavior and these may include:
